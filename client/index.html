ecoPlantly


Growing your own food at home is not only easier than it looks, but it can also help save the environment, one green onion at a time.


No idea where to start? Don’t worry, you’re not alone. ecoPlantly is here to be the guide for your home gardening journey.

our mission


Growing your own food geatly cuts down on carbon emissions compared to buying from the grocery store. To help home gardeners be more conscious of the environmental impact of food sourcing, ecoPlantly tracks and visualizes how much home gardeners are saving in carbon emissions with each vegetable they grow.


We want to bridge the gap between inexperienced gardeners and home gardening resources. Growing your food is truly accessible. ecoPlantly provides a beginner-friendly database of vegetables and herbs, complete with considerations for available space, climate, and materials. Trust us, anybody can garden if they really want to!

the background


Food production alone accounts for one quarter of the world’s greenhouse gas emissions. The majority of food’s carbon footprint can be attributed to mass production emissions in factories and farms. Transport to grocery retailers is also a huge factor, with the average distance travelled by retail vegetables being 1500 miles.


Growing your own food reduces production resources consumed, organic and packaging waste, and food miles travelled, all of which are significant contributors to greenhouse gas emissions. If households could replace just 20% of store-bought food with self-grown food, they could reduce their carbon footprint by 68 pounds of CO2e a year!
